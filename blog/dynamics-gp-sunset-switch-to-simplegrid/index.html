Dynamics GP Sunset: Switch to SimpleGrid

May 26, 2026 Industry Critique / Product 6 min read

Microsoft set the clock. Here's how to move - without betting 18 months and six figures on it.

If you run a manufacturing or distribution business on Microsoft Dynamics GP, you already know this is coming. Microsoft has set the dates, and they are not soft.

  • April 2025 - no new perpetual GP licenses.
  • April 2026 - no new GP sales of any kind. That date has passed.
  • December 31, 2029 - mainstream support ends. No more product updates. No more tax and regulatory updates.
  • April 30, 2031 - security patches stop.

Read the 2029 line again. After that date, your year-end close freezes on the 2029 tax ruleset. After 2031, an unpatched financial system sits on your network. That is not a risk you manage. It is a date on a calendar.

So you have to move. The only real question is how.

The default answer - and the trap inside it

Walk into any Microsoft partner and you'll hear the same answer: migrate to Dynamics 365 Business Central.

It's not a bad product. But look at what that project actually is. Twelve to eighteen months. Six figures before you're live. Data cleanup, re-customization, testing, training, change management - all of it. And at the end, you've bent your business to a new general-purpose ERP.

That's the same trade GP users made fifteen years ago.

Here's what gets forgotten: GP didn't work because it was great out of the box. It worked because, over fifteen years, you made it yours. Custom fields. SmartList views. ISV add-ons. Macros. Reports nobody else has. Your GP install became a record of how your business actually runs.

A generic re-platform throws all of that away. You're not modernizing. You're re-buying the same kind of system and re-doing the same customization grind - this time against a deadline.

What GP actually got wrong

GP's general ledger was never the problem. For straight financials, it did its job.

The problem was everything bolted on around it. The floor. Work orders tracked in Excel because the GP module didn't fit your process. Approvals routed over email. Production status living in a supervisor's head. Three add-ons stitched together to do one thing your business does every day. The accounting was fine. The operations layer never bent to the way you run.

A switch to another general-purpose ERP doesn't fix that. It just resets the clock on the same fight. "Best practices" is the part you should be suspicious of.

The SimpleGrid answer

SimpleGrid is an AI-native ERP built on one principle: you don't adapt to the system. The system adapts to you.

AI reads your operation - your work-order cards, your Excel sheets, your GP exports - and writes the schema for your business. Not a module you configure. A system shaped to how you already work. Every factory floor is different. That's the whole point.

Deploy in 7-14 days. Not 18 months.

And here is the part that matters for anyone who's been burned by an ERP project before: zero deployment cost. We build it on your data, at our cost. You run it for 30 days. You pay only if you keep it. You see it working on your own operation before you spend a dollar.

If it doesn't fit, you walk away. No invoice. No sunk six figures. The deadline didn't force you into a bad system.

Keep your books where they are

We're not here to rip out your general ledger and tell you to trust us with payroll on day one.

SimpleGrid runs operations - production, inventory, work orders, approvals, the floor. Accounting integrates: QuickBooks, Xero, or your existing books. You modernize the layer GP never handled well, and you keep financial continuity where you need it.

That's the honest version. It's also the lower-risk one.

Is this for you? We onboard selectively.

SimpleGrid is not for everyone leaving GP, and we'd rather say so up front.

It fits if you're an operator-led manufacturer or distributor, roughly $6M-$600M in revenue, with GP running your floor and no real IT department to absorb an 18-month project. Custom furniture, fabricated metals, apparel, food processing, distribution - that's the core.

If you're a pure multi-entity finance shop and GP's GL is the center of your world, Business Central may genuinely be your path. We'll tell you that on the first call. We onboard the businesses we can make succeed, not every business we can sign.

Don't let a deadline pick your system

The 2029 date is doing Microsoft's selling for it. Panic pushes good operators into the fastest "safe" answer - and the fastest safe answer is usually the most expensive one.

You have time to choose well. Use it. Pick the system that proves itself on your operation before you commit, not the one with the biggest migration invoice.

The GP sunset is real. Your next eighteen months and six figures don't have to be.
