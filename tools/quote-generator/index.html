What makes a good manufacturing quote

  • Validity date. Material prices move; lock your number to a window (30 days is standard).
  • Lead time clearly stated, with "ARO" (After Receipt of Order) so the clock starts on PO, not on quote acceptance.
  • Payment terms. Deposit + balance protects you on custom work where you can't easily re-sell if the customer walks.
  • Shipping terms (FOB origin vs destination). Determines who pays freight and when title transfers.
  • Acceptance signature line. Turns the quote into a contract once signed.

FAQ

Quote vs estimate vs proposal - which is this?

This is a quote - a firm price commitment, time-limited. An estimate is informal and non-binding. A proposal usually includes broader scope and terms beyond just price.

Should I include a signature line?

For custom work, yes - turns the quote into a contract once signed. For commodity items, optional.
