Why annual physical inventory is broken

It shuts production down for 1-2 days. It exhausts your team. And the result is still 85-92% accuracy because hundreds of items get counted in a hurry by people who do not know them. Then the count holds for about 30 days before drifting back to where it was.

Why cycle counting works

Counting a small portion of inventory every day, focused on the items that matter most, produces 98%+ accuracy over time without ever stopping production. The discipline shifts from one annual cleanup to a daily habit. Discrepancies get found and investigated in real time, not weeks later.

The frequency strategy

A items (20% of SKUs, 80% of value): count monthly. B items: quarterly. C items: annually. The total annual count workload usually fits in 1-2 hours per day.

Frequently asked questions

How accurate can cycle counting get?

98% within 12 months is standard. 99%+ achievable in 18-24 months with discipline. Industries that require it (aerospace, medical) routinely hit 99.5%+.

Can cycle counting replace my annual physical?

Yes - IRS Revenue Procedure 98-25 allows it if you can demonstrate accuracy meets standards (typically 95% or better). Talk to your accountant.

Do I need barcodes?

Not strictly, but they help enormously. Manual counting is error-prone; barcode scanning eliminates most counting errors. ROI on a basic scanner system: typically 6-12 months.

Who should do the counting?

Trained warehouse staff is ideal. Some manufacturers rotate the responsibility to ensure no single person can mask errors. Crucial: counter should NOT also be the receiver - separation of duties matters.

What if I find lots of discrepancies?

Good - now you know. Root-cause every one: receiving error, unrecorded scrap, mislabeled location, backflush error, shipping error. These five causes account for 70-80% of discrepancies in mid-market manufacturing.
