The three methods, in one sentence each

FIFO (First In, First Out): COGS uses the oldest costs - matches physical flow (oldest material used first). Higher inventory value on the balance sheet. Higher COGS-driven profit during inflation.

LIFO (Last In, First Out): COGS uses the newest costs - tax-favorable during inflation (lower profit = lower tax). Balance sheet shows old, often understated inventory values. Not allowed under IFRS.

Weighted Average: Smooths everything to one average cost. Simplest to maintain. No tax advantage either direction. Allowed under both GAAP and IFRS.

Which method most US manufacturers use

FIFO is the default for most mid-market manufacturers. LIFO has tax advantages in inflationary periods but adds complexity and is rarely worth it below $1M in annual material spend. Weighted average is common in process industries (chemicals, food, commodities) where the physical mixing makes FIFO impractical.

Frequently asked questions

Why does method matter if my inventory is the same physically?

Because the accounting assumption about WHICH units were used affects COGS, which affects taxable income. Same physical reality, different reported profit.

Should I switch to LIFO for the tax savings?

Maybe. Run the numbers. If your annual material spend is below $1M, the complexity probably is not worth it. Above $5M with rising commodity costs, LIFO can save real money - but talk to your accountant about IRS Form 3115 first.

What is the LIFO conformity rule?

If you elect LIFO for tax purposes, you must also use LIFO for your audited financial statements. You cannot show high profit on financial reports and low profit to the IRS.

Why is LIFO prohibited under IFRS?

IFRS regulators decided LIFO does not reflect actual inventory flow and can be used to manipulate earnings (LIFO liquidations can artificially boost profit). The IRS still allows it because it generates real economic effects.

What happens to LIFO reserve when prices fall?

It shrinks. If you have built up a large LIFO reserve over years of rising prices and then prices fall, you can recognize "LIFO liquidation" gain - which is taxable. Surprise to many manufacturers.
