What a supplier scorecard does that gut feel cannot

Most manufacturers run on relationships: "I think they are reliable." A scorecard turns that into "they hit 94% on-time delivery, 0.6% reject rate, respond in same-day, ISO 9001 certified." When a critical supplier starts slipping, you see the trend in the data months before the relationship goes off the rails. ISO 9001:2015 Clause 8.4 expects you to evaluate suppliers formally - a scorecard is the lightweight way to comply.

Five dimensions, not just two

Quality (30%): rejects, certifications, CAPA response. Delivery (25%): on-time, lead time consistency, advance notice on delays. Commercial (20%): price, terms, hidden costs. Service (15%): responsiveness, flexibility, account management. Risk (10%): financial, geographic, BCP, capacity. Weighting puts quality and delivery at 55% of the score - because that is where most production-line damage actually happens.

Rating tiers map to action

A (90+): strategic partner - lock in long-term agreement. B (75-89): preferred - schedule QBRs, target weak dimensions. C (60-74): approved but needs improvement - start qualifying alternatives. D (45-59): probationary - formal CAR, 60-day improvement plan. F (<45): exit plan - replace within 90 days.

Frequently asked questions

How often should I score suppliers?

Critical/high-criticality suppliers: quarterly. Standard suppliers: annually. The most-disciplined manufacturers schedule QBRs (quarterly business reviews) for tier-1 suppliers where they walk through the scorecard together.

What weighting is right for my business?

The defaults (Q30/D25/C20/S15/R10) reflect typical mid-market manufacturing priorities. Adjust if your business is unusual - e.g., regulated industries weight Quality higher (35-40%), highly time-sensitive operations weight Delivery higher.

Should I share scorecards with suppliers?

Yes - that is where the value compounds. A supplier who sees a B and the path to an A will work for it. A supplier who never hears feedback assumes everything is fine until you switch.

What if I disagree with a sub-score?

Override it. The framework is a scaffold, not a verdict. The scorecard exists to make implicit judgments explicit, not to override your operational knowledge.

Is this ISO 9001-compliant?

It satisfies Clause 8.4 (Control of Externally Provided Processes), which requires documented evaluation criteria, ratings, and re-evaluation. Keep the PDF on file as evidence; auditors typically accept this format.
