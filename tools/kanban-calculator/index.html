Why kanban beats MRP for stable parts

MRP plans every part at every level on a calendar. It works when demand is forecastable and supplier behavior is reliable. For most mid-market manufacturers, neither holds for most parts. The result: MRP keeps re-planning, schedules churn, and inventory grows because the "safety stock" parameter keeps getting bumped up.

Kanban replaces planning with signaling. When a card returns, you make or buy that container. No forecast, no calendar. It works perfectly for high-frequency stable parts and poorly for low-frequency unpredictable parts. The split point is usually around 30-50 SKUs in a typical mid-market shop.

When NOT to use kanban

Parts that move less than once per month - waste of system complexity, use min/max. Parts with demand variability above 50% coefficient of variation - kanban will stock out frequently, use forecasted MRP. Parts with long, variable supplier lead times - the kanban quantity becomes large enough that it stops being kanban and starts being safety stock.

Frequently asked questions

How big should a container be?

About 1/10 of daily demand is a good starting point. Smaller containers = smoother flow but more handling. The floor is whatever your physical handling system supports (bin size, tote weight, forklift load).

What safety factor should I use?

10-20% for stable demand, 30-50% for variable demand. Above 50% suggests kanban is the wrong tool - the part is too unpredictable.

What goes into lead time?

Order placement + supplier production + transit + dock-to-stock inspection. Time the entire signal-to-shelf path. Most kanban failures come from underestimating internal delays (paperwork, inspection queues).

How often should we resize?

Quarterly. Add a card after any stockout. Remove a card after 90 days of no stockouts and no near-misses. Document the trigger so the system stays defensible.

Can we use kanban for everything?

No. Use it for high-frequency stable parts (typically A and B items by usage frequency). For low-frequency or highly variable demand, use min/max or MRP. Hybrid is normal.
