Why pipeline-stage weighting beats gut forecasting

Most manufacturers forecast by feel: "about the same as last quarter, maybe up a bit." That works until it does not. The mid-market disasters are almost always forecasting failures - capacity built for revenue that did not materialize, or material shortages because growth was not predicted. Stage-weighted forecasting attaches a probability to each opportunity based on where it is in your sales process. The total weighted pipeline is your realistic forecast.

Stage probabilities for manufacturing

Defaults reflect MAPI Foundation data: 10% for inquiries, 20% for RFQ-requested, 35% once you have submitted a quote, 50% in negotiation, 75% on a verbal commit, 95% with a PO. Adjust these based on YOUR conversion rates if you have the data. Most shops who track this find their stage rates are tighter than they expected once they have submitted a quote.

The pipeline coverage rule

Healthy pipeline coverage: 3x your revenue target for the quarter. Less than 2x is fragile - you cannot afford any opportunities to slip. More than 5x usually means you are not closing efficiently. The 3x rule comes from typical B2B sales conversion rates - you need 3 dollars of pipeline to deliver 1 dollar of revenue.

Frequently asked questions

How is a pipeline-weighted forecast different from a regular forecast?

Pipeline-weighted means each opportunity is multiplied by the probability it closes based on its stage in your sales process. A $100K opportunity at "quote submitted" stage (35% probability) is worth $35K in your weighted forecast - not $100K. This produces realistic numbers, not best-case ones.

What is a good pipeline coverage ratio?

3x your quarterly revenue target is the standard rule. Below 2x is fragile; above 5x usually means inefficient closing. The 3x rule reflects typical B2B conversion rates - you need $3 of pipeline for every $1 of expected revenue.

How accurate is a stage-weighted forecast?

For mid-market manufacturing with a 6-month forecast horizon, expect +/-15% accuracy if your stage probabilities are calibrated against your own history. The first 60-90 days are most accurate; months 4-6 widen to +/-30%.

What if I do not know my stage probabilities?

Use the manufacturing-industry defaults (MAPI): 10% inquiry, 20% RFQ, 35% quoted, 50% negotiating, 75% verbal commit, 95% PO. Then track your actual win rate per stage for 6-12 months and replace the defaults with your real numbers.

Why does my biggest customer represent so much of the forecast?

Customer concentration risk in the pipeline. If your top opportunity is over 25% of weighted forecast, you cannot hit the quarter if it slips. Diversify your pipeline by closing more, smaller deals or adding bigger ones to balance.
