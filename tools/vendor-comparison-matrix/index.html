Why "go with my gut" loses procurement decisions

Most vendor selections happen in 30-minute meetings dominated by whoever speaks last. Price wins by default because it is the only number everyone agrees on how to compare. Quality, delivery, service, capacity - all the things that actually decide whether the relationship works - get hand-waved. The matrix exists to make every dimension explicit, weighted, and visible side-by-side.

How to use the weights

Weights reflect what matters to YOUR business, not generic best practice. A regulated industry weighs Quality at 9-10 and Tech Capability at 8. A commodity buyer weighs Price at 9 and Tech at 3. A capacity-constrained business weighs Capacity / Scalability at 8. There is no universal right answer - which is exactly why the matrix forces you to make weights explicit and defensible.

Use it for documentation

The PDF output is procurement documentation. ISO 9001 auditors expect documented vendor selection rationale; this satisfies that requirement. So does any internal financial audit. Run the matrix, save the PDF, file it. The next vendor decision happens with the prior decision's rationale on the table.

Frequently asked questions

How many criteria should I use?

6-10 is the sweet spot. Fewer than 6 misses key dimensions. More than 10 dilutes everything and the math stops differentiating.

What weight should I give price?

For commodity items: 8-10. For engineered or complex products: 5-7. Price almost always feels more important than it should be - quality and delivery typically have larger downstream economic impact.

How do I score vendors I have not worked with?

Use samples, references, supplier visits, and quote/RFI responses. For untested vendors, default to 3 (average) and adjust as you learn more. The matrix is more useful as a structured recording of what you know than as a predictor of unknowns.

Can I customize the criteria?

In this version, the 8 criteria are fixed. Adjust the weights to emphasize what matters to you - that captures most of the customization need without making the tool unwieldy.

Should I share the matrix with vendors?

Generally no during selection - it can create gaming. But after selection, sharing the criteria with the chosen vendor sets clear expectations for performance review.
