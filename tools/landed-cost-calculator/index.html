FOB to landed: the gap most manufacturers do not see

FOB Shanghai $1.50 sounds cheap. By the time the container clears customs in Long Beach, gets trucked to your warehouse in Akron, and gets received, that $1.50 part is $2.10 - sometimes $2.25 or more. The buildup is mechanical: freight $0.18, duty 5% on customs value, Section 301 25% on China origin, broker $200/shipment, port + drayage $850, inland freight $800, insurance 0.4% of CIF, plus receiving labor. None of these are negotiable individually; together they swing your sourcing decision.

Section 301: the tariff that changed everything

Since 2018, most Chinese-origin manufacturing goods carry an additional 7.5-25% tariff on top of the regular duty rate. For a 5%-base-rate product, that means total duty of 30%, not 5%. Many manufacturers built their cost structures before Section 301 and never updated them. The numbers no longer work the way they did.

When domestic wins on landed cost alone

The break-even point is roughly 25-30% above FOB China for most manufacturing commodities. If a domestic supplier can match that, they often win - especially when you add the TCO components landed cost ignores (lead time, inventory, quality, risk).

Frequently asked questions

What is Section 301?

The tariff added by the Trump administration in 2018 on most Chinese-origin manufacturing imports. Rates range from 7.5% to 25% depending on the HTS code list. Applied on top of regular duty. Subject to change - check ustr.gov.

How accurate is this calculator?

Within ~10% for standard ocean shipments with stable rates. Less accurate during disruption periods (post-COVID 2021-2022 ocean rate spike, Red Sea routing 2024) where freight rates swung 3x. For binding numbers always get a quote from your customs broker.

Should I include duty drawback?

Not in this calculator (it would over-credit). If you re-export goods made with imported materials, you can recover up to 99% of duties paid via duty drawback. File within 5 years.

What is MPF and HMF?

Merchandise Processing Fee (0.3464% of value, $31.67-$614.35) on most formal entries. Harbor Maintenance Fee (0.125% of value) on ocean shipments. Both are real costs that go into landed.

Why is my landed cost so much higher than my supplier quoted?

Suppliers quote FOB or EXW (free on board / ex works). Everything from the supplier dock to your dock is your cost - and there is more of it than most buyers realize. That gap is exactly what this calculator surfaces.
