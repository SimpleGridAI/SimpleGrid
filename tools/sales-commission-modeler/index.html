Commission structure is not just a percentage - it is a behavior incentive

The choice between flat revenue, tiered revenue, gross-profit-based, and tiered-margin commission plans is the choice of what behavior you want from your reps. Flat revenue commission rewards volume regardless of margin - reps will discount aggressively to close deals. Gross-profit-based commission aligns rep behavior with company profitability - they fight for margin because they get paid on it. Most mid-market manufacturers default to flat revenue commission, then wonder why their margins erode quarter over quarter.

The right plan for your business

First sales hire, simple product line: flat revenue. Easy to administer, rep understands it instantly.
Variable margins across products: margin-based. Otherwise reps cherry-pick the highest-volume, lowest-margin deals.
Mature sales team, sophisticated buyers: tiered margin. Highest alignment with business goals; needs transparent margin data per deal.

Benchmarks that matter

Manufacturing sales comp: avg base $65K-$85K, avg OTE $100K-$150K, avg commission 3-8% of revenue OR 10-20% of gross profit. Top performers earn 1.5-2.5x OTE. Base/variable split: 60/40 is the manufacturing norm (MAPI). Cost of sales runs 4-8% of revenue for direct selling, 8-15% for complex/long-cycle work.

Frequently asked questions

What is a typical manufacturing sales commission?

Avg manufacturing sales rep: 3-8% commission on revenue OR 10-20% on gross profit. Outside sales: $100K-$150K OTE typical. Inside sales: $70K-$100K OTE. Top performers reach 1.5-2.5x OTE.

Should I pay on revenue or gross profit?

Gross profit-based plans align rep behavior with company profitability - they fight for margin. Use revenue-based plans only for simple, consistent-margin product lines or for a first sales hire learning the role.

What is a good base/variable split?

Manufacturing norm: 60% base, 40% variable (commission/bonus) at OTE. For new business hunters: shift to 50/50 or 40/60. For account management: 70/30 base-heavy. The split signals how much risk you want the rep to bear.

What is OTE?

On-Target Earnings - the total compensation a rep earns at 100% of quota. Includes base salary plus expected commission/bonus when targets are hit. The number you advertise when recruiting.

How do I set quota?

Standard rule: 5-10x the rep OTE in revenue (i.e., a $125K OTE rep should generate $625K-$1.25M in revenue). Below 5x, your cost-of-sales is too high. Above 10x, the quota may be unrealistic and demotivating.
