PPV: the procurement KPI most mid-market manufacturers do not track

Purchase Price Variance compares standard cost (what you budgeted) to actual price paid (what you paid). Unfavorable PPV - paying more than standard - is the single fastest way to erode gross margin without any visible cause. Most mid-market manufacturers do not track PPV because their ERP does not enforce standard costs. So material costs drift, margins shrink, and nobody knows why until quarter-end.

How to use PPV

Set standard costs at the start of the year (or each quarter). Track actual prices on every PO. Compare. Items with unfavorable PPV >5%: re-quote, get competitive bids, consider alternate suppliers. Items with favorable PPV: lock in with a blanket order before the price moves. Categories with persistent unfavorable PPV: structural problem, address upstream.

Standard cost maintenance

Your standard costs are useless if they are stale. Items where actual is more than 10% off from standard distort job costing and pricing. Update standards quarterly for high-spend items; annually for everything else.

Frequently asked questions

What is a favorable vs unfavorable PPV?

Favorable (negative variance): you paid LESS than standard - margin booster. Unfavorable (positive variance): you paid MORE than standard - margin drag.

How big should PPV be?

Best-in-class procurement: PPV within +/-2% of standard across the portfolio. Average mid-market: 4-7%. Above 10% means standards are stale, supplier relationships are sloppy, or both.

Should I update standard costs when prices change?

Yes - for high-impact items, quarterly. But not for every fluctuation; the point of "standard" is a stable reference. Update when the gap becomes structural (3+ months above 5% variance) not noise.

How does PPV affect my job costing?

If your standards are wrong, every job quote you build is wrong. If actual material cost is consistently 8% above standard, your quoted margins are 8% higher than your real margins. PPV reveals exactly this gap.

What is the difference between PPV and material price variance?

Same thing. "PPV" is the term used in procurement; "Material Price Variance" is the cost-accounting term in standard-costing systems.
