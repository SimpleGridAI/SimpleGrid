Consignment is not always a good deal

The supplier sales pitch sounds great: "we will stock the material at your facility, you only pay when you use it." Zero inventory investment, no carrying cost. The catch: consignment prices are typically 5-15% higher than standard purchase prices. Whether consignment actually saves you money depends on whether the carrying-cost savings exceed the price premium - which depends on your cost of capital, your inventory levels, and your storage costs.

When consignment wins

High-value items where carrying cost is large relative to total cost. Long-lead-time items where consignment eliminates stockout risk. Capital-constrained businesses where freed working capital matters more than 8% of inventory value. Items with stable usage (volatile usage destroys consignment economics).

When standard purchase wins

Low-cost commodity items where carrying cost is small. Volatile-price items where the consignment premium gets stuck above market. Items where obsolescence risk is real and the contract has not nailed down who bears it.

Frequently asked questions

What is consignment inventory?

Supplier-owned inventory stored at the buyer facility. Buyer only pays as they consume it. Supplier retains ownership (and obsolescence risk, ideally) until use.

What is a typical consignment price premium?

5-15% over standard purchase price, reflecting the supplier cost of carrying inventory and the working-capital benefit they are providing the buyer.

Who insures consignment stock?

Contract-dependent. Best practice: supplier insures while in their ownership. Many contracts inappropriately push insurance onto the buyer - read carefully.

What happens to consignment stock if I end the relationship?

Contract-dependent. Best practice: supplier removes their stock at their cost. Avoid contracts that require you to buy out existing consignment inventory on termination.

How often should I count consignment stock?

Monthly is standard for high-velocity items. Quarterly is acceptable for stable, low-turnover items. The counting cost is real - factor it into the consignment math.
